As required, top up the power-steering fluid to the proper fill level. If your car’s cylinder has gradations, gradually add the fluid until you reach the desired “hot” or “cold” fill level; if you used a dipstick to verify the level, add the fluid slowly to prevent overfilling the reservoir.

What is the best way to remove power steering fluid?

Remove the lowest power steering line you can locate, which is usually near the steering gear, rack, or cooling lines. Allow for thorough draining of the system, which may take some time owing to the viscosity of power steering fluid. After the system has been emptied, fill the power steering reservoir to 12 percent capacity.

What happens if I mix brake fluid with power steering fluid?

Damage to the brake system occurs as soon as a petroleum-based fluid, such as power steering fluid, enters the system. The reservoir cap and master cylinder seals are usually the first to be damaged. What happens if your power steering fails?
