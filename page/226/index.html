NOVA | Volcano's Deadly Warning | Anatomy of a Volcano: Dike

published on August 16, 2024
Dike Dikes are tabular or sheet-like bodies of magma that cut through and across the layering of adjacent rocks and then solidify. They form when magma rises into a fracture or creates a new crack by forcing its way through rock. Hundreds of dikes can invade the cone and inner core of a volcano, often along zones of structural weakness.
