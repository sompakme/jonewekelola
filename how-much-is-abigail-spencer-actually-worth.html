How Much Is Abigail Spencer Actually Worth?

Pascal Le Segretain/Getty Images By Ribhu/April 27, 2021 5:58 pm EST

Abigail Spencer has had an illustrious career: She started off back in 1998 on ABC, starring in the daytime soap opera "All My Children," for which she won the Soap Opera Digest Award for Outstanding Newcomer. There was no looking back from there, and she took one role after another. Her movie debut was "Campfire Stories" in 2001.

Over the years, Spencer went on to have great parts in shows like "Angela's Eyes" and "Mad Men." In 2013, she starred in the drama series "Rectify," for which she was nominated for two awards; she was also featured in Season 2 of "True Detective," the critically-acclaimed series that won multiple Emmys. She's also starred in "Suits," "Timeless," "Grey's Anatomy," and more. In fact, Spencer is known for her friendship with "Suits" co-star Meghan Markle. She was even present at Meghan's wedding to Prince Harry in 2018. Their bond has grown strong enough to the point where Spencer wrote a heartfelt post on Instagram in Meghan's support during public drama with the royal family.

Abigail Spencer's net worth complements her success in TV and film. So how much is Spencer worth, with an exciting career spanning over two decades?

Abigail Spencer has a comfortable net worth

Frazer Harrison/Getty Images

There's no doubt that Abigail Spencer has starred in a number of high-profile TV shows, as well as movies that have performed well at the box office — like "Oz The Great And Powerful," which grossed $493 million worldwide. According to Taddlr, Spencer's yearly salary is half a million dollars. As for her overall net worth, Celebrity Net Worth reports that the actor is worth $3 million.

Notably, Spencer also has a side hustle — as a florist. As she told The Kit, flowers "are the symbolism of what we do with our pain. We can invest in the soil and make something beautiful or it can destroy us. ... I want people to buy flowers for themselves. When I think about my mission, it is an act of self-care."

Spencer's paychecks were enough to allow her to purchase a $1.3 million luxury real estate in Studio City, Los Angeles. According to a March 2016 report from the Los Angeles Times, Spencer's 1923 Spanish-style house features arched doors and windows, a master suite with a soaking tub, and tiled ceilings. The 1,700 square foot interior expands to accommodate three bedrooms and two bathrooms and a formal living room that features a chandelier, fireplace, and built-in bookshelves.
