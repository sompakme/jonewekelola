Who is Allen Stone from American Song Contest?

NBC's hit reality show American Song Contest. saw Washington's Allen Stone battle it out with ten other artists

Let's take a closer look at the contestant.

Who is Allen Stone?

Allen Stone is an American soul and R&B musician.

Born March 13, 1987, Allen describes himself as "a hippie with soul," and his music reflects both sides of this formula.

The Chewelah, Washington, native lived in Seattle for a few years before making the move to Washington.

The singer began cross-country touring in 2009, promoting a self-released, digital-only album that sold briskly.

His career began kicking into high gear in October 2012 when he released his second digital album self-titled Allen Stone.

After being backed by artists including retro-soul star Raphael Saadiq and jazz keyboardist Deron Johnson, Allen made appearances on the Conan O'Brien and David Letterman shows.

He was later featured on the song Neon Cathedral by Macklemore and Ryan Lewis off their album The Heist.

In 2013, he appeared on The Ellen DeGeneres Show and sang Sleep.

He soon became signed to ATO Records, a New York label co-founded by Dave Matthews.

How did Allen Stone do in American Song Contest?

On April, 11, 2022, Allen took to the stage in heat four of American Song Contest, representing Washington State.

After singing A Bit Of Both, he topped the jury vote and went straight through to the semi final of the competition.

Three more contestants from the heat will also advance to the semis, with voting lasting three days.

The three acts will come from either Massachusetts, Nevada, Pennsylvania, West Virginia, Georgia, Utah, New Hampshire, Hawaii, Arizona and Washington DC.

The results will be announced at the beginning of heat five, which is the final heat in the competition before the semi-finals.

Heat five airs on Monday, April 18, 2022, at 8pm ET/7pm CT.

What are Allen Stone's best songs?

The song Allen performed on American Song Contest, A Bit Of Both, was an original piece.

Other songs by Allen include Brown Eyed Lover, Sunny Days, Consider Me and Unaware.
