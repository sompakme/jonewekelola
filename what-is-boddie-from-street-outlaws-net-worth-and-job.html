What is Boddie from Street Outlaws net worth and job?

Boddie, a prominent figure on “Street Outlaws” and a participant in the latest No Prep Kings season, has been making waves in the world of street racing. Fans are curious about his net worth and what he does when he’s not burning rubber on the track. In this article, we’ll delve into Boddie’s career and reveal the numbers behind his success.

Boddie’s Professional Background:

Boddie is a seasoned street racer hailing from California’s Bay Area, and his passion for racing spans several decades. He is the owner of Team Boddie Racing, a sports promoting business dedicated to the world of racing. Interestingly, he is the third youngest son of Kenny, a well-known figure in the racing scene who used to race alongside his brother Ronnie.

Despite his love for basketball, Boddie’s determination led him to save enough money to purchase his very first race car, a 1970 Nova, at the young age of 19. His impressive driving skills have earned him a place among the top-rated street racers. Boddie continues to organize races across the West Coast and beyond, showcasing his unwavering commitment to the sport.

A Glimpse into Boddie’s Past:

Before his rise to fame on “Street Outlaws,” Boddie reportedly had a different source of income. From 1999 to 2001, he was involved in the drug trade, providing him with a means of livelihood during those years.

Boddie’s Net Worth:

Boddie’s net worth is estimated to be at least £1 million. This substantial fortune is a result of various income streams, including his role on the Discovery show “Street Outlaws” and the operation of Team Boddie Racing.

To put it into perspective, most of the cast members on “Street Outlaws” earn salaries ranging from $20,000 to $30,000 per episode, and this doesn’t even account for race winnings. Boddie’s participation in the show has undoubtedly contributed significantly to his wealth.

Comparatively, his fellow “Street Outlaws” cast member Ryan Martin boasts a net worth of $2 million, while business owner Robin Roberts is believed to have a net worth of less than $1 million.

Boddie’s Racing Achievements:

In 2010, Jermaine Boddie made the pivotal decision to fully commit to racing, leading to the official launch of Team Boddie Racing. This transition marked the culmination of a lifelong dream nurtured by his experiences watching his father and uncle race during the 60s and 70s.

In 2012, he acquired his current racing car, a 1963 Nova, which became his trusted companion on the track. Boddie’s exceptional driving skills propelled him to the ranks of top-rated street racers.

He made a mark at grudge race tracks across the United States, leaving his footprint in places like Georgia, Texas, Nevada, and his home state of California. Notably, in 2014, Boddie organized his inaugural annual grudge race event titled “No Excuses,” drawing drivers from all corners of California and nearly 1,000 enthusiastic spectators.

It was this very event that caught the attention of “Street Outlaws” producers, leading to the chapter of his life that is now part of racing history.

My Final Opinion, Boddie’s journey from a passionate street racer to a prominent figure on “Street Outlaws” is a testament to his dedication and love for the sport. With a net worth that reflects his success, Boddie continues to make waves in the world of street racing, leaving a lasting legacy for fans and racers alike.
