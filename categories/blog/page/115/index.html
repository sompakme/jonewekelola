The First Black Female Lawyer In America Was Born In The 1800s

published on September 08, 2024
In 1879, Charlotte E. Ray moved back to New York City, New York. After seven years of keeping a legal practice, both racist and gendered discrimination kept her from maintaining a successful practice, according to "Women in American History." Upon moving back to New York City, Ray resumed her teaching career in the Brooklyn public school system where her sister Florence Ray reportedly worked as well.
