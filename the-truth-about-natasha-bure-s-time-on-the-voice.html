The Truth About Natasha Bure's Time On The Voice

Appearing as a contestant on "The Voice" was not without its scarier moments for Natasha Bure. In February, Natasha reflected on this experience while speaking on the "Conversations with Olivia Jade" podcast, per People. The performer explained that advice from her mother, "Full House" star Candace Cameron Bure, motivated her to go on the competition series when she was in her late teens. "The only reason I went on was because there was this thing that my mom told me â€” she's like, 'If there's an opportunity that comes and it scares you, you should say yes just because it scares you,'" she said.

Natasha added that she "had the worst nerves of [her] life" and "sobbed [her] eyes out" before singing on "The Voice," which resulted from being judged by a larger audience for the first time. "It really terrified me to know everyone is going to watch this," Natasha said. Though she revealed she's now "mortified" by her "The Voice" performances, Natasha called this "a good growing experience" in her career.

Since then, Natasha has continued to share her vocal talents publicly, including posting a 2019 Instagram video of herself belting out "If This is Love" by Ruth B. In addition, she has followed her other passion of acting.
