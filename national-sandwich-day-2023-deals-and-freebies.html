National Sandwich Day FAQs

Got more questions about National Sandwich Day? Read more below.

What Is National Sandwich Day?

National Sandwich Day is an unofficial holiday recognized by people and food service providers across America. It's like other food-based unofficial holidays, including National Cheese Day (June 4) and National Cookie Day (December 4). You won't get a day off work for National Sandwich Day, but you might get a free sandwich.

The holiday celebrates the sandwich, supposedly invented by John Montagu, 4th Earl of Sandwich. Legend goes that Montagu asked for a slice of roast beef between two pieces of bread, so he could eat it with his hands. However, we don't know if this is what really happened or if it happened on November 3.

When Is National Sandwich Day?

National Sandwich Day happens on November 3 every year. In 2023, it falls on the first Friday of November.

When Is National Chicken Sandwich Day?

National Fried Chicken Sandwich Day is on November 9. Don't confuse it with National Sandwich Day, which applies to all sandwiches.
